Malawi Integrated Household Survey

This page accompanies the audio recording dataset. It describes what was asked in each interview, in what order, and how the questionnaire was structured — so researchers can understand the content of recordings without listening to them.

This is a phone-based household panel survey conducted in Malawi in coordination with the Living Standards Measurement Study team at the World Bank. It is administered by trained enumerators using a tablet application. All interviews were conducted in Chichewa. The questionnaire shown here is the second official draft.

Employment

Present in Rounds 1 and 2. Asked of the main household respondent, and also the second respondent in Round 2. The module routes differently depending on whether the respondent was working at the last interview.

Three routing cases The module routes differently depending on the respondent's work status at the previous interview. Case 0 — respondent was not interviewed last time; Case 1 — respondent was working at last interview; Case 2 — respondent was not working at last interview. Food Insecurity Experience Scale

Present in Round 2. Administered to a randomly selected individual aged 15 or older per household, and also to the second respondent. The enumerator selects which individual using the tablet — this selection is not spoken aloud. All questions expect a yes/no answer.

Static text Two introductory sentences are read aloud verbatim before sub-sets of questions. These will appear in audio before questions 1 and 6.
  • Tablet randomly selects a respondent aged 15 or older to participate in this module.
  • Tablet checks whether the selected individual is 15 or older and whether they are the randomly selected participant.
  • spoken
    Is [NAME] answering for himself/herself?
  • spoken — static text
    "During the last 30 days, was there a time when, because of a lack of money or other resources, you experienced the following:"
  • Q1
    Were worried you would not have enough food to eat?
  • Q2
    Were unable to eat healthy and nutritious food?
  • Q3
    Ate only a few kinds of foods?
  • Q4
    Had to skip a meal?
  • spoken — static text
    "Still thinking about the last 30 days, was there a time when, because of a lack of money or other resources, you experienced the following:"
  • Q5
    Ate less than you thought you should?
  • Q6
    Ran out of food?
  • Q7
    Were hungry but did not eat?
  • Q8
    Went without eating for a whole day?

Education

Present in Round 2. Asked about each household member aged 3–18 years. The questionnaire notes this was intended to be collected once every six months. School level classifications (Q5) were marked as requiring country-specific adaptation before deployment.

Interviewer note After completing each visit, the enumerator records general observations about the interview for supervisors and analysis purposes.
  • Tablet checks whether the individual is aged 3–18 — not spoken aloud.
  • Q1
    Is [NAME] answering for himself/herself?
  • Q2
    Who is responding for [NAME]? [ID code recorded]
  • Q3
    Is [NAME] attending school in the [current] school year?
  • Q4
    What is the main reason [NAME] is not attending school during the [current] school year?
  • Q5
    What level is [NAME] attending during the [current] school year? [Country-specific adaptation needed]
  • Q6
    During the last week that school was open for lessons, was [NAME] absent from school on any day?
  • Q7
    How many days was [NAME] absent from school during the last week? [response in days]
  • Q8
    What is the main reason [NAME] was absent from school?

Shocks and Coping Strategies

Present in Round 2. Asked of the main household respondent. The module asks about adverse events the household has experienced since the last interview, what impact those events had, and how the household responded.

Interviewer preamble (spoken aloud): "I would like to ask you about events that occurred since [LAST INTERVIEW DATE] and may have affected your household."

  • Q1
    Since the last interview on [LAST INTERVIEW DATE], did your household experience any of the following events?
    Record YES/NO for each event first, then proceed with the rest of the module.
    DroughtsFloodsCyclones / hurricanes / typhoons / tornadoesHeatwavesWildfiresInsect infestations / pest and disease outbreaksHuman health crisis / epidemic / pandemicConflict / local unrest / violenceSubstantial increase in commodity prices
  • Q2
    Since the last interview on [LAST INTERVIEW DATE], did your household receive any warnings about the risk of [SHOCK]?
  • Q3
    When [SHOCK] occurred, did any of the following happen to your household?
    Read options aloud. Select YES/NO for each.
    Reduction / loss of incomeLoss of crop before harvestLoss of livestockDisruptions to farming / household business activitiesInjuries / casualties of household membersDestruction of or damage to household dwelling / buildings / facilitiesLoss or destruction of other household assets including landForced displacement / relocation
  • Q4
    How did your household cope with the consequences of [SHOCK]?
    Do not read options — respondent answers freely.
    open-ended
  • Q5
    In addition, did your household do any of the following to cope with the consequences of [SHOCK]?
    Read options aloud. Select all that apply. Options already given in Q4 are excluded.

  • Q1
    Did you or anyone in the household cultivate any land, either owned or used by the household, in the last completed main agricultural season, from [START MONTH/YEAR] to [END MONTH/YEAR]?
  • Tablet checks whether the household previously cultivated land in the last in-person interview.
  • Q2

    Select all that apply.
  • Q3

  • Q4
    What were the challenges your household encountered?
    Select all that apply.

    What is the total area of land cultivated by your household in the last completed main season?
    Area and unit recorded — local units permitted.

Livestock

Present in Round 2. Asked of the main household respondent. Questions cover three areas: what livestock the household owns, challenges with accessing inputs and services, and revenues from selling animals and animal products.

  • Q1
    In the past 12 months, has your household owned or kept any livestock?
  • Q2
    What type of livestock does your household own or keep?
    Select all that apply.
  • Q3
    What challenges did your household face in your livestock activities?
    Select all that apply.
  • Tablet checks whether "reduced access to animal feed" was selected in Q3 before asking Q4.
  • Q4
    What was the main way your household coped with the reduced access to animal feed?

  • Tablet checks whether "reduced access to breeding material" was selected in Q3 before asking Q5.
  • Q5

Mental Health (Patient Health Questionnaire)

Present in Round 2. Administered to both the main respondent and the second respondent. Eight items from the PHQ-8 depression screening scale. All eight questions use the same four-option response scale.

Response scale — read aloud before all items "In the next set of questions, please answer 'Not at all' or 'Several days' or 'More than half the days' or 'Nearly every day'. Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by any of the following problems?"
Not at allSeveral daysMore than half the daysNearly every day

    Little interest or pleasure in doing things
  • Q2
    Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
  • Q3
    Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much
  • Q4
    Feeling tired or having little energy
  • Q5
    Poor appetite or overeating
  • Q6
    Feeling bad about yourself — or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down

  • Q8
